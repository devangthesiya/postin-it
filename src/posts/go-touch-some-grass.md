Ever wondered how much social media consumes you and your mental peace?

Not just if you're consuming it, but even if you're creating (and yes, it affects creators too, maybe even more). I'm not one of them, but you can still sense it if you pay attention.

No matter how much you try not to compare yourself with others, you will. No matter how much you try to avoid certain people or content, you just can't. And honestly, that isn't even the main problem with social media.

The bigger issue is what it does to your attention span. It slowly brings it down to a point where your mind starts craving something new every 30–60 seconds. A new story, a new reel, a new emotion. You keep swinging from one feeling to another without even realizing it, and somewhere in between, your mind just gets… tired.

---

I've been away from it for about a full week now, and genuinely speaking, it's been going well. Now when I open my phone, there are very limited apps left to browse, which is fine honestly. Not a big deal. If anything, it's saving a lot of my time.

Some of that time goes into better sleep (okay, not always 😄), but yeah. I've been playing chess a bit more these days. Surprisingly, my YouTube screen time has gone up, which was expected, but I'm okay with it. It actually feels like I'm back to how I used to use it around 2018–19.

What I watch on YouTube… we'll get to that some other time. Not today. Won't bore you with that.

---

The point is simple.

Don't stress your small dimaag with so many small, small stories that it starts frying at one point.

As people from our generation would say—

go touch some grass. 🌱
