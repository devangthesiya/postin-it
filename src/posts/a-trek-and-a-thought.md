Went trekking this Saturday night. It was a sunrise trek.

We left the city around 12, and by 1:30 the bus had picked up almost everyone. It was one of those quiet rides where conversations slowly fade and people fall asleep one by one. I stayed up for a while, just looking outside, watching empty roads and streetlights pass by. It felt like the kind of plan that would have been better if she was there, sitting next to me, probably talking about random things or just resting quietly. But you don't always wait for the right company. Sometimes you just go.

We reached the base around 3:40 after a short break in between. There wasn't even a forecast of rain, but it still started pouring, and from the beginning till the end, it stayed with us like a constant companion.

I didn't have a raincoat or anything, so there wasn't much to do except just accept it. At some point during the climb, I caught myself thinking how she would have reacted. She would have complained for sure, maybe blamed me for not checking the weather properly, but she would have still smiled through it. That thought stayed with me longer than the rain did.

The trail was wet and a bit slippery, but somehow that made it more fun. There were a few spots where I paused for a second, not because I had to, but because I could almost picture her there, trying to balance, maybe slipping once or twice, and laughing about it. It's strange how clearly you can imagine someone in places they've never been.

We reached the top around 5:30. It was still raining, and the wind was strong enough to make it difficult to stand still. For a while, it didn't even feel like a place you could stay in for long. But slowly, the rain began to settle.

And then the sky started to change.

The clouds opened up just enough for the sun to break through. It was red. Not slightly orange or soft yellow, but properly red — the kind you don't really see every day. For a moment, everything else just faded a little.

And without thinking much, I looked around, almost like I expected her to be there too.

That's the thing about views like this. You don't just want to see them. You want to turn to someone and say something about it, even if it's nothing important.

We stayed there for around two hours, just sitting, walking around, letting time pass without really keeping track of it. It was peaceful, but it didn't feel complete. Not in a sad way, just in a quiet, almost unnoticeable way.

After that, we made our way back down and eventually reached home by around 12 in the afternoon.

It was a good trip. The kind you're glad you took.

But somewhere in between the rain, the climb, and that red sunrise, it felt like there was a version of this day that would have been just a little better.
